Description

Manufactured by many companies, one of the most prestigious was the Barnett in London.

It was used in the navy and in the cavalry. The model that is reproduced, was at the service of the Empire of Brazil in the Paraguayan War (1865-70).

If someone looks for this model of pistol in the catalogs of the royal armory is not going to find it or will it find it in a section where it is mentioned that it belonged at West Indies since the English had used to make weapons according to the place where they were going to exercise their dominion. There was an East Indian version very similar to this model but different from this in that the knob or finish is flat and has a fixed ring giving the impression of being a shorter gun.

Features

Enfield Pistol (Barnett)
Barrel: steel, exterior, conical, smooth interior (as its original)
Caliber: .58
Lock: Percussion
Carriage: walnut
Guards: polished brass
Ramrod: poling
Weight: approx. 1350 gr.
