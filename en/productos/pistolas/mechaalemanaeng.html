German Matchlock Pistol Code: PM001

<%= require("fs").readFileSync(htmlWebpackPlugin.options.filePath + '/compartir.html') %>

Description

Until not long ago, the only references to short matchlock weapons were linked to Japan. Further more, there are publications that dared to say that matchlock guns were not built in the West.

In fact, it occurred to us that in many photographs and descriptions when the “little arquebuses” are mentioned, more than once, they could be referring to a pistol.

But the true image of a matchlock pistol itself came into our hands as these things happen, by chance.

It is a pistol built in approximately 1650, which on the barrel and plate bears the inscription SLV, which after the 17th century will become SUHL.

Like 90% of current reproductions from other companies, this model does not follow in detail the measurements described in the historical model, but this is not as noticeable in this model, as it is in some reproductions of other models made by famous companies of the rest of the world.

Features

Enfield Pistol (Barnett)
Barrel: two ordered, plain interior, with target points
Caliber: .50
Lock: Matchlock
Stock: walnut
Guards: steel
Weight: 1100 gr.
