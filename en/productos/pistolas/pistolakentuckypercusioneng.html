Description

Kentucky pistol with percussion. c. 1830. This is the same pistol with spark that pioneered the independence of the United States, later transformed to incorporate the piston or percussion system, which made it more reliable and more accurate, although it continued to retain its fame with the previous system, with flint.

Features

Percussion Kentucky Pistol
Barrel: steel, octagonal outside, knurled inside
Caliber: 45 or 50
Lock: Percussion
Carriage: walnut
Guards: polished brass
