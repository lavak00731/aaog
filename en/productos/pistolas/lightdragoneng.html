Description

Replica of the famous English Ligth dragoon pistol, military line weapon used by the officers in almost all the lands where the English were present. In the case of our country it is the pistol that they brought in the English invasions produced between the years 1806 and 1807, being the model of the end of the XVIII century the most diffused at that time

However, there are some examples from 1805, already veterans of the Trafalgar period, where the English confronted the French naval forces, with a very strong cannon and an extraordinarily safe key, the walnut carriage gives a solid appearance to this pistol, which can often be seen with a metal ramrod, although in reality most of them were made of wood with a bronze tip. Cavalry examples have also been seen using a rigged ramrod to prevent the ramrod from being lost in the heat of battle, and the plate is engraved with the British crown and the word tower, which in popular parlance ended up giving the gun its name. In reality this means that the gun was the property of the crown and that it had passed through the Tower of London for inspection.

Features

Light Dragoon Pistol
Barrel: smooth steel
Caliber: .64
Lock: flintlock
Carriage: walnut
Guards: polished brass
Weight: approx. 1200 gr.
