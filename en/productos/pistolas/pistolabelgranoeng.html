Description

Handmade, with the same materials, same size, exact decorations and engravings and the same functionality, as the pistol that the Cabildo of Buenos Aires gave to our hero in 1814.

It was built entirely by hand, taking care of every aesthetic and FUNCTIONAL detail.

Each component is made in our Workshop of Construction of Antique Guns, in the City of Rosario, Santa Fe, Argentina. It has no intervention of third parties, companies, or purchased parts... each screw, each piece and finishing, is made by us, referencing at all times what has been achieved, with the original gun.

It is a limited edition model, where each Tribute Set will consist of the following: A fine polished wooden box (of our own manufacture), lined in black corduroy, which on its lid exhibits a bronze plaque with the signature of General Manuel Belgrano. Inside, a handful of lead spherical ammunition and some sharp flints, accompany the Pistol with an engraved plaque naming the Procer, origin of the original weapon and year of delivery. Finally, a full color booklet (also by our author), telling briefly the history of the Original Pistol, our visit to the museum to document it and passages of the construction of the gun in question.

NOTE: This exclusive item is not part of the rest of the Catalog exhibited on this website, which is available for order on a constant basis... This is an article designed to be produced to order as well, but with a LIMITED number of units to be manufactured.

Features

General Manuel Belgrano's Pistol
Barrel: smooth steel
Caliber: 16 mm
Lock: flintlock
Carriage: polished walnut
Guards: polished brass
Engraving: low-denomination silver fall on the barrel
Weight: approx. 900 gr.
