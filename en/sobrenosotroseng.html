Introduction

A replica is a copy or reproduction of something original, respecting shapes, materials, usefulness, weight, characteristics and operation.

My name is Osvaldo Gatto and when I have to define who I am and what I do, I like to say that I am a history teacher, always in love with old weapons and within this field, muzzleloader pistols. By the way, the weapons of all times have served as the axis when it comes to understanding the domination that one people exercised over another, the technological development achieved or the cultural concept that was revealed as a characteristic of the time studied. In this, the invention of gunpowder came to mark "with fire" supremacy or obedience depending on the side in which it was. Thus, black gunpowder, of uncertain origin, was the propellant in firearms, long or short, artillery, naval or fortified, until the end of the 19th century when the use of nitrocellulose gunpowder put it on the plane of the old everything done before.

These jewels from the past, military or civilian, large or small, with spark or percussion, with engravings or "peeled", sober or refined, always caught my attention, with an inexplicable seduction from the countless showcases. grooved or smooth, as if sleeping, as if they were volcanoes. For something even more inexplicable, I always felt like I was. I felt the strong impulse to reproduce them, as if some artisan of antiquity had awakened genetically in my blood, preparing myself for the sometimes difficult task of transforming a massive steel pipe a barrel or a piece of wood into a pistol stock. A wooden gun on a gun carriage, a spring strap or any apparently inert material that forms part of a piece that, although new, keeps the essence of an original. Something like that, saving the distance from nonsense, as occurs with science that tries to clone dinosaurs or other disappeared animals.

I tried from where I was able to approach originals and study them, some by purchase, others by loan, by model or period, by system, etc. and reproduce them effectively and realistically making it clear that my goal is never to pass off a current reproduction as something from the past. From there So my replicas have never pretended to pass for anything else, as expressed by their markings. The result of patient work, each pistol can take between 45 and 60 days to develop. This brings me to a perhaps idle clarification: it is impossible to talk about stock, so my work is almost always on request. However, what seems against it can be turned in favor because the non-existence of a series production allows me to reproduce perhaps unique models at ease. like this It is also interesting what is done with regard to restoration, scale models or some models of bladed weapons, with a preponderance of the Renaissance that I build from scratch.

Workshop

From the selection of the ideal piece of wood to the final finishing and polishing of each one of the parts, there is a long way to go. The secret of the entire process is to ensure that each element, no matter how small, has its own well-defined characters. The result is a piece with a unique personality.

The work in our workshop is extremely varied and covers a wide range of well-defined processes and activities.

Depending on the type of construction that each piece requires, be it a cannon, a dagger, a dagger or a pistol, there are very clear stages to follow. The maximum and absolute care in each one of them is of the utmost importance so that the final finish has the forcefulness and quality that characterize our products.

Each pistol has its genesis in a piece of wood that will later pass into the future. It became a gun carriage thanks to careful work based on file task. The final finish reaches its maximum splendor thanks to lacquers, varnishes and resins.

Whether it is the blade of a dagger, the barrel of a pistol or the core of a dagger, the nobility of the steel is something to take into account when selecting the material to start manufacturing a piece. Polishing and finishing are done entirely by hand.
